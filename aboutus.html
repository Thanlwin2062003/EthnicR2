Ethnic Resilience


About Our Organisation

Established in 2023, Ethnic Resilience (EthnicR) is a non-profit Myanmar civil society organization (CSO) committed to providing assistance to vulnerable ethnic communities wherever they exist. EthnicR was established in 2021 as a loosely organized collective of volunteers operating in Myanmar's Myawaddy and Kawkayate regions. The organization with a particular emphasis on women and children directed its efforts towards aiding vulnerable people living in remote camps and villages along the Mie River. These ethnic communities have long suffered due to a protracted civil war, and their situation has only deteriorated as a result of the violent national military coup of February 2021.

A Governing Committee consisting of 9 persons elected democratically by its General Body governs SGJSS. Elections on the board are held every five years. SGJSS runs on a fully decentralized management system headed by the Secretary, who is supported by a cadre of professional and administrative staff. Program and performances are monitored on a regular basis in quarterly review and planning meetings. Eminent experts from the field of management, agriculture, environment, health, women empowerment, and social work, etc. are invited to these meetings.

Organisational structure

Acknowledging the disproportionate impact of disasters on marginalized and displaced communities, EthnicR is dedicated to responding to community-identified concerns, such as education, primary healthcare, and other essential emergency support with an emphasis on mothers, children, the elderly, and disabled individuals.

Vision

EthnicR is currently shifting into a new operational phase.

Education in Emergencies

While EthnicR has always provided training and education, the organization is in the process of formally adopting relevant aspects of the ‘Minimum Standards for Education, Preparedness, Response, Recovery’. EthnicR accepts that the provision of education helps to ensure equitable, and inclusive learning opportunities for individuals of all ages in crisis situations, spanning from preparedness to response and recovery through various types of education, from primary, secondary, up to vocational. By providing education in emergencies (EiE), EthnicR has the added opportunity of enhancing community engagement through the provision of vital information on lifesaving and survival skills and providing essential services such as protection, nutrition, water, sanitation, hygiene, disability support, and health can be delivered, simultaneously building community resilience.
